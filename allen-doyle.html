Allen Doyle (Golfer) - On This Day

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Profession: Golfer

Biography: Allen Doyle is an American golfer who has played on several major tours including the Nike Tour, PGA Tour, and Champions Tour. He has won four PGA Senior major titles, as well as the US Senior Open in 2005 and 2006.

Doyle played amateur for a number of years and won many titles but did not turn professional until he was 46. He then entered into the Senior PGA Tour in 1998 when he turned 50, in which he has had great deal of success.

Born: July 26, 1948
Birthplace: Woonsocket, Rhode Island, USA
Age: 76 years old

Generation: Baby Boomer
Chinese Zodiac: Rat
Star Sign: Leo

Playing Career

  • 1999-04-18 Senior PGA Championship, PGA National GC: Allen Doyle wins first of 4 Senior PGA major titles with a 2 stroke margin over runner-up Vicente Fernández of Argentina
  • 2001-07-15 Senior Players Championship Men's Golf, TPC of Michigan: Allen Doyle wins his 2nd Champions Tour major with par on first extra hole of a playoff with Doug Tewell
  • 2004-08-29 The Tradition Senior Men's Golf, Reserve Vineyards & GC: Craig Stadler wins his final major event by 1 stroke from Allen Doyle and Jerry Pate
  • 2005-07-31 US Senior Open Men's Golf, NCR CC: Allen Doyle wins by 1 stroke ahead of Loren Roberts and D. A. Weibring for his 3rd Champions Tour major title
  • 2006-07-09 US Senior Open Men's Golf, Prairie Dunes GC: Defending champion Allen Doyle wins by 2 strokes ahead of Tom Watson for his 4th Champions Tour major title
